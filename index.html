2019

StackAdapt

Homepage, WordPress theme, HTML5 emails + creatives

StackAdapt is one of the fastest-growing tech startups in Toronto, with an innovative native advertising platform that enables brands to accelerate customer engagement and acquisition.

In 2017, they hired me as a Front End Engineer to give much-needed focus to their homepage and blog.

At their rate of growth, they saw a need to build a digital marketing team and elevate their online presence. I worked closely with the Design and Marketing teams to revamp their homepage, introducing new product pages, industry pages tailored to specific verticals, an Ad Format Showcase, and a lot more.

Working at StackAdapt enabled me to grow my skills by learning WordPress admin and development, and to focus on the needs of an in-house marketing team. One of the first initiatives that I led was a migration of their old blog to WordPress, including the development of a custom WordPress theme.

I also built responsive email templates, landing pages and other tools for their marketing automation platform, which helped take their communication and customer experience to new heights.

Over the 2 years at StackAdapt, Steve has brought to life many complex projects. His is a talented developer who doesn't settle for “average” and always pushes the envelope to create impactful beautiful products. His team-first, positive attitude made it very easy to work with him to execute on deliverables even with tight deadlines!

Vitaly Pecherskiy

COO, StackAdapt

2017

Diply

Rapid prototyping, UX design, front end development

Diply was one of the leading social news/entertainment websites in the world and among the top 20 most visited websites in Canada, publishing articles on internet culture, food, fashion, celebrity news, user-generated content and much more.

In 2016, as they began staffing their brand new Toronto office, they took me on as a developer to augment their Design team.

At the time, Diply was in the design stages of creating self-publishing tools and user dashboard web applications, as well as experimenting with new designs for their homepage. Using Node/SASS/jQuery, I built rapid prototypes alongside the designers, which we used for presentation demos, design review and user testing.

Later in my role at Diply, I continued working on these tools as part of their Angular dev team, building UI components for the final product.

I started working with Steve my first week at Diply after I had already heard from others that I would love prototyping with Steve. His background in art and design made him a favourite amongst designers.

During his time at Diply we worked together on creating quick proof of concept prototypes as well as the suite of tools that writers, account managers and influencers use on a daily basis. Steve always surprised by bringing something unexpected to the work that made it that much better.

Developers who pay attention to the details and the overall experience are always a pleasure to work with and this was Steve for me. Our white board sessions were always rewarding as Steve would often help guide the solution to the next level. His ability to ask the right questions constantly pushed me as a designer to really think about things from a developer’s perspective.

I have worked with many front-end developers and I will say that Steve’s passion and enthusiasm for the details is not often matched so I hope that our paths cross again in the near future.

Rich Auger

Director of Design (Product & Brand), Diply

2016

Infusion

Rapid prototyping, front end / UI development

Infusion (now Avanade) is a global engineering, design and digital strategy firm with an emphasis on solutions built with Microsoft technologies. Their clients included retail, education, healthcare, financial services, the energy sector and others. Their projects ranged from customer-facing digital experiences to enterprise-level application software.

In 2013, their client, Richtree Natural Markets, was developing a brand-new food court concept at the Toronto Eaton Centre. Infusion was tasked with providing digital ordering touch screens, overhead menus and interactive installations for the new food court space.

With my prior experience in XAML, I was hired to develop UI for the various Windows WPF applications that powered these experiences.

Later, as Infusion was incubating a digital marketing agency within the firm, I continued as a member of their Interactive Development team, a multidisciplinary group of design-aware, marketing-savvy creative technologists, developers and designers.

In my time there, I helped produce innovative and exciting digital experiences for various clients, pitch work, rapid prototypes, UX design, front end and UI development on various platforms including: Web, Windows, Android, Samsung Gear wearables, Unity, Microsoft Hololens and more.

Working at Infusion was a wonderful experience for me, as I got to learn and play with many new technologies and to build experiences on various platforms, particularly VR/AR.

I had the pleasure of working shoulder to shoulder with Steve on one of the harder projects of my career and he was an incredible team mate. He was instrumental in bringing the project to life with his passionate front end integration skills. He was quick to learn anything needed to complete the project, and was very willing to collaborate and figure out compromises to challenging in the name of a better experience. I would gladly work with Steve again.

David Stubbs

Senior Graphic Designer, Infusion

2013

nventive

Front end / UI development

nventive is an agile mobile and web application studio, which in 2012-2013 was designing and building apps on Windows Phone and Windows 8 devices.

At the time I started there, Microsoft was investing heavily in the Windows Phone app ecosystem, and nventive was one of the studios that produced full-featured Windows Phone and Windows 8 apps for clients such as ABC News, AllRecipes, Bloomberg, Expedia and others.

Their need for a Windows XAML front end developer was so urgent, and the hiring pool so small, that they hired me based on my skill level with HTML/CSS and started training me on XAML development on the first day. After two weeks, I was competent enough to work on production codebases, and with the rest of my time there, I learned much more.

I introduced a practice of producing app-level style guides early in the design process, which outlined colour resources, font sizes and styles, etc. helping the Design and Development teams work together more effectively.

When Steve started at nventive, I was flabbergasted on how quick Steve was learning new technologies. He did learn XAML and Windows Phone language in a few days! He is rigorous in his work and always ready to improve the process or the work cycle. A good team member and a great sense of humor!

Mathieu Hétu

Agile Product Manager, nventive
